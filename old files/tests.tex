If $\nabla f_i(x_t) = \nabla f(x_t) + \epsilon_t$,
$$\epsilon_t = (\phi_i \phi_i^\top - H)(x_t-x^*)$$

$$\mathbb E (\epsilon_t) = 0$$ 
$$\cov (\epsilon_t) = \mathbb E [(\phi_{i_t} \phi_{i_t}^\top - H)(x_t-x^*)(x_t-x^*)^\top (\phi_{i_t} \phi_{i_t}^\top - H)] = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n (\phi_{i} \phi_{i}^\top - H)(x_t-x^*)(x_t-x^*)^\top (\phi_{i} \phi_{i}^\top - H)$$

Supposition: $\cov(\epsilon_t) \preceq C$, and $H$ and $C$ are codiagonalizable. $C = \diag(c_1,...,c_n)$

$x_{t+1}^{i} = x_t^i - \eta_t\lambda_i(x_t^i - x_*^i) + \epsilon^i$

\begin{align*}
